Frequently Asked Questions

DevOps is the combination of development (Dev) and operations (Ops) to enhance efficiency, high-end security, and the entire software development cycle more efficiently. DevOps methodology utilizes the culture of philosophies, best practices, and tools that speed up overall development time while ensuring top-quality products in the first place. The main goal of DevOps is to create a sustainable infrastructure for specific applications to help ensure higher scalability, reliability, and productivity. After all, scalable apps are highly reliable, automated, and integral in the modern-day software development process.

DevOps is a set of practices, tools, and a cultural philosophy that combines both development (Dev) and operations (Ops) teams, allowing them to work together to build, integrate, automate, test, and deploy software with a faster time to market. Thus, you will see significant improvements in team empowerment, cross-team collaboration and communication, and technology automation.

The main benefit of adopting DevOps automation is that it gives you faster, reduced complexity, and better product delivery in stable operating environments. DevOps automation allows you to work smarter, and faster while ensuring higher productivity and availability. Furthermore, DevOps automation eliminates overall complexity and errors more efficiently.

There are several strategies you can add or implement to ensure zero downtime within your CI/CD pipelines, including but not limited to proper planning & coordination, automation testing, blue-green deployment, canary deployments, and performance monitoring. Hiring talented DevOps engineers can help ensure zero downtime.

CI and CD is the process of automating the integration of code changes from various developers into a single codebase. The main goal of CI/CD is to streamline and accelerate the software development lifecycle while ensuring top-quality code in the first place.
