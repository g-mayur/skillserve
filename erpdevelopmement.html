Frequently Asked Questions

ERP stands for Enterprise Resource Planning, a software that allows companies to integrate numerous business functions and processes, including HR, finance, supply chain, inventory management, etc. into one unified, integrated comprehensive system.

The main features of a robust ERP system include powerful modules or extensions for customer relationship management (CRM), finance, human resources, reporting or analytics, and supply chain management. To make sure your ERP system is fully integrated and functionality-rich, be sure to check out these key features.

An ERP system provides various advantages to businesses, such as streamlined processes, automated repetitive tasks, enhanced efficiency, better data management, faster decision-making, and more. With functionality-rich ERP software, companies can gain better data insights, digital presence, and excellence in the digital world.

The cost of building an ERP system with basic features and functionality may range anywhere from $10,000 to $30,000. However, the cost of building and implementing a complex ERP system with customization capabilities may range somewhere between $50,000 and $650,000 for SMEs and large business enterprises.
